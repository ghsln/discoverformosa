Managing Money in Taiwan

Taiwan offers a modern and convenient financial ecosystem for travelers. While digital payments are increasingly popular, cash remains important for many local experiences. Understanding your payment options will help you navigate Taiwan smoothly and enjoy everything from high-end shopping to traditional night markets.

Payment Methods

Taiwan accepts a wide variety of payment methods, making it easy for travelers to manage their expenses.

EasyCard & iPass

These rechargeable smart cards are essential for travelers in Taiwan. Beyond transportation, they work at:

  • All MRT systems and city buses
  • Convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Hi-Life, OK Mart)
  • Vending machines
  • Some restaurants and shops
  • YouBike rentals
  • Parking meters

Credit Cards

Widely accepted at:

  • Hotels and major accommodations
  • Restaurants and cafes
  • Department stores and shopping malls
  • Chain stores and supermarkets
  • Online bookings for trains and attractions

Mobile Payments (Apple Pay & Google Pay)

Increasingly popular and accepted at:

  • Stores with contactless payment terminals
  • Major retail chains
  • Convenience stores
  • Coffee shops and modern restaurants

Cash (NTD - New Taiwan Dollar)

Still essential for:

  • Night markets and street food
  • Small local shops and traditional markets
  • Taxis (though some accept cards)
  • Rural areas and small towns
  • Temples and donation boxes

Important Payment Tips

  • Always carry some cash for small shops and local markets
  • Convenience stores accept both cards and mobile payments
  • Night markets typically only accept cash
  • Foreign cards may not work everywhere - have backup options
  • Small denominations (NT$100, NT$500) are useful for markets

ATM Machines & Cash Withdrawal

ATMs are easily accessible throughout Taiwan, making it convenient to withdraw cash whenever needed.

Where to Find ATMs

  • Convenience Stores: 7-Eleven and FamilyMart have ATMs available 24/7
  • Banks: Taiwan Bank, Mega Bank, Cathay United Bank, and others (usually open during business hours)
  • MRT Stations: Major stations have ATMs in the station areas
  • Airports: Available in arrival and departure halls
  • Shopping Areas: Found in malls, department stores, and tourist districts

Using ATMs in Taiwan

  • Most ATMs accept international cards (Visa, Mastercard, Plus, Cirrus, UnionPay)
  • Look for ATMs with English language options (most modern ATMs have this)
  • Daily withdrawal limits typically range from NT$20,000 to NT$30,000
  • Convenience store ATMs may have lower limits (around NT$20,000)

ATM Fees

  • Local bank fees: Usually NT$100-150 per withdrawal
  • Your home bank may also charge international withdrawal fees
  • Check with your bank before traveling about foreign transaction fees
  • Consider withdrawing larger amounts less frequently to minimize fees

ATM Safety Tips

  • Use ATMs in well-lit, secure locations
  • Cover the keypad when entering your PIN
  • Keep your receipts and check your account regularly
  • Notify your bank before traveling to Taiwan to avoid card blocks

Where to Exchange Currency in Taiwan

Discover the top places to exchange currency in Taiwan in 2025. From banks to airports, find the best rates and convenient locations for your Taiwan adventure!

Despite the popularity of electronic payments in Taiwan, cash is still essential, especially in night markets and small shops where cards often aren't accepted. Foreign cards may also be unreliable, so carrying some Taiwanese dollars is important. While you can prepare money before your trip, exchange rates outside Taiwan are usually worse because the Taiwanese dollar isn't widely circulated. This makes withdrawing or exchanging money after arriving in Taiwan the most cost-effective option. Luckily, Taiwan offers several convenient ways to get local currency, making it easy to stay prepared during your trip.

Best Places to Exchange Currency

1. Banks (Best Rates)

  • Generally offer the most competitive exchange rates
  • Major banks: Bank of Taiwan, Mega Bank, Cathay United Bank, First Bank
  • Operating hours: Monday-Friday 9:00 AM - 3:30 PM
  • Some branches open on Saturday mornings
  • Bring your passport (required for exchange)

2. Airport Exchange Counters

  • Available at Taoyuan, Taipei Songshan, and Kaohsiung airports
  • Open long hours including early morning and late evening
  • Convenient for immediate needs upon arrival
  • Rates are typically 1-3% less favorable than banks
  • Good for exchanging small amounts for initial expenses

3. Hotels

  • Available for registered guests
  • Most convenient but typically offer lower exchange rates
  • Best used for emergency situations
  • Not all hotels provide this service

4. Licensed Money Changers

  • Found in tourist areas, shopping districts, and near major attractions
  • Rates vary - compare before exchanging
  • Ensure they are licensed and authorized
  • Extended operating hours compared to banks

Currency Exchange Tips

  • Exchange rates in Taiwan are typically better than in your home country
  • Always bring your passport - it's required for currency exchange
  • Compare rates at different locations before exchanging large amounts
  • Consider withdrawing from ATMs for competitive rates with 24/7 convenience
  • Exchange enough cash for immediate needs (2-3 days), then use ATMs as needed
  • Keep exchange receipts for your records
  • USD, EUR, JPY, and CNY are the most easily exchanged currencies

Money Management Tips for Taiwan

Before You Travel

  • Notify your bank and credit card companies about your Taiwan trip
  • Check your daily ATM withdrawal and spending limits
  • Understand your bank's foreign transaction fees
  • Consider getting a travel-friendly credit card with no foreign transaction fees
  • Download banking apps for easy account monitoring

During Your Trip

  • Carry a mix of payment methods: cash, cards, and mobile payments
  • Keep small bills (NT$100, NT$500) for markets and street food
  • Use EasyCard/iPass for daily transportation and convenience store purchases
  • Save ATM receipts and monitor your accounts regularly
  • Keep emergency cash separate from your main wallet

Understanding Taiwan Currency (NTD/TWD)

  • Coins: NT$1, NT$5, NT$10, NT$50
  • Bills: NT$100, NT$200, NT$500, NT$1,000, NT$2,000
  • NT$2,000 bills can be difficult to break at small shops
  • Most commonly used: NT$100, NT$500, and NT$1,000 bills

Typical Daily Expenses

  • Budget meal: NT$100-200
  • Mid-range restaurant: NT$300-600
  • Convenience store snacks: NT$30-100
  • MRT fare: NT$20-65
  • Taxi (short ride): NT$100-300
  • Hostel/budget hotel: NT$600-1,500 per night
  • Mid-range hotel: NT$2,000-4,000 per night

Final Recommendations

  • Start with NT$3,000-5,000 in cash for the first few days
  • Use ATMs to withdraw more cash as needed
  • Keep your EasyCard/iPass topped up for smooth transportation
  • Use credit cards for larger purchases and hotels
  • Always have at least NT$500-1,000 in emergency cash
