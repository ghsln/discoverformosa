Cherry Blossoms in Taiwan

Cherry blossoms in Taiwan bloom mainly from January to March, turning mountain areas and parks into popular seasonal attractions, especially in places like Yangmingshan, Alishan, Wuling Farm, and Sun Moon Lake. Influenced by Japan during the colonial period, cherry blossom viewing (hanami) has become a beloved spring activity, symbolizing renewal, beauty, and the arrival of warmer weather.

Different Types of Cherry Trees

Yoshino Cherry

The most famous variety with delicate pale pink blossoms that create stunning pink clouds when in full bloom. Yoshino cherries are often planted along streets and in parks, creating picturesque avenues of pink.

Formosan Cherry

Native to Taiwan, this variety features deep pink to red blossoms and is particularly hardy. It's commonly found in mountainous areas and blooms slightly earlier than other varieties, often starting in late January.

Double-Flowered Cherry

Known for their lush, multi-layered deep pink blossoms that create a particularly dramatic display. These trees bloom a bit later in the season and retain their flowers longer than single-petal varieties.

Mountain Cherry

Thrives in higher altitudes with vibrant pink blossoms that contrast beautifully against the green mountain backdrop. These are especially spectacular in Taiwan's national parks and scenic areas.

Weeping Cherry

Characterized by drooping branches covered in pink blossoms, creating a waterfall effect of flowers. These are less common but create stunning focal points in gardens and parks.

Map for Viewing Sites

Explore the best cherry blossom viewing locations across Taiwan. The bloom typically starts in the southern regions and moves northward, with higher altitude locations blooming later in the season.

Best Viewing Spots & Season

Yangmingshan National Park, Taipei

February - April

One of the most famous spots, with cherry trees lining the roads and covering the hillsides. The park becomes a sea of pink during peak bloom, attracting thousands of visitors for hanami (flower viewing) parties and photography sessions.

Alishan National Scenic Area

March - April

A magical experience with cherry blossoms set against a backdrop of misty mountains and ancient forests. The combination of cherry blossoms with the famous Alishan sunrise and forest railway creates an unforgettable scenic experience.

Wuling Farm (武陵農場), Taichung

February

Known for its stunning, dense forests of cherry trees, it's one of the most picturesque and popular spots. The farm features various cherry varieties that bloom in succession, creating an extended viewing season within the area.

Season Overview: Generally from late January through April, moving from the south to the north and from low to high altitudes. The cherry blossom front moves up the island, allowing for extended viewing opportunities across different regions.
