Natural Disasters in Taiwan

Taiwan's location makes it prone to natural events like earthquakes and typhoons. However, the country is exceptionally well-prepared with robust infrastructure, effective emergency systems, and clear public safety protocols. Understanding what to do before, during, and after these events will help ensure your trip remains safe and enjoyable.

Important Note: Taiwan has one of the world's best disaster preparedness systems. Buildings are constructed to withstand earthquakes, and typhoon protocols are well-established. While awareness is important, major risks remain low for travelers.

Earthquake Preparedness

Taiwan sits on the Pacific Ring of Fire, so earthquakes happen from time to time. Most are small and rarely cause problems, and the country is well-prepared with strong buildings and effective emergency systems. While it's good to be aware, travelers can enjoy their trip knowing that safety is taken seriously and major risks are low.

Practical Teaching: Learn the Self-Protection Technique in 3 Steps

Following Japanese earthquake safety practices, here's what you need to know:

1Daily Preparation

Prepare a Disaster Prevention Go Bag:

  • Water (bottled)
  • Dry food and snacks
  • Flashlight
  • Power bank (external battery)
  • Emergency medicine
  • Simple raincoat
  • Cash
  • Copies of important documents

Stay Informed:

  • Follow Taiwan's Central Meteorological Administration Earthquake Information Website
  • Join Telegram channels like @taiwanalert for real-time warnings
  • Note: Travelers may not receive earthquake early warnings on their mobile phones

Know Your Location:

  • Check escape exits when staying in hotels
  • Ask Airbnb owners for safety information
  • Locate evacuation points nearby

2During an Earthquake

Remember: Drop, Cover, Hold On

This is the universally used earthquake self-protection formula:

  • Drop: Avoid standing, lower your center of gravity, reduce the risk of falls
  • Cover: Immediately hide under a sturdy table or protect your head with your hands/pillow
  • Hold On: Hold the cover tightly and move only when the shaking stops

3What NOT to Do

⚠️ NEVER During an Earthquake:

  • Rush out of the house
  • Take the elevator/lift
  • Stand near windows
  • Stand on balconies
  • Stand near collapsible buildings

These are the most dangerous actions during an earthquake!

Safety Tips: How to Prepare Before Traveling

If you are preparing to visit Taiwan, remember that disaster prevention is just as important as planning attractions:

Before You Go:

  • Follow Official Sources: Central Meteorological Bureau, @taiwanalert Telegram channel
  • Avoid High-Risk Areas: Be cautious in Alishan, Jianan Plain, mountain roads, and loose geological areas
  • Choose Stable Accommodation: Recommended to stay in newer buildings and low-rise units
  • Check Escape Exits: Whether staying in a hotel or Airbnb, always do your safety homework

Typhoon Season Guide

Planning a trip to Taiwan during typhoon season? Taiwan's typhoon season usually runs from June to October, bringing heavy rain and strong winds at times. While most tourist areas are prepared and resilient, travelers should stay updated on weather forecasts and follow local guidance during storms. Typhoons are part of the island's climate, but with a little planning, trips can still be safe and enjoyable.

1. Typhoon Alerts & Safety Tips for Travelers

Central Weather Administration (CWA, Taiwan)

What it offers:

  • Real-time typhoon track maps
  • Rainfall radar & alerts
  • English updates
  • Marine warnings & forecast animations

Website: Central Weather Administration

Typhoon Alert Apps

Features:

  • Push notifications for typhoon alerts
  • Heavy rain warnings
  • Uses official CWA data
  • English available

iOS: TW Typhoon Tracker (App Store)

Android: Taiwan Weather (official from Central Weather Administration)

Social Media & Local Alerts

Useful Sources:

  • Facebook: Taiwan News, Focus Taiwan (CNA English) - Both post frequent typhoon and weather updates
  • LINE App: Taiwan's main messaging platform. Many cities/counties offer official LINE alert accounts (some in English)

2. Preparing for a Typhoon: Step-by-Step Guide

Essential Items to Pack

  • Lightweight raincoat or poncho – Easy to carry and keeps you dry during sudden showers
  • Waterproof shoes – Important for walking through wet streets or light flooding
  • Portable power bank – Power outages can happen, so keep your devices charged
  • Emergency contact list – Include your hotel, embassy, and insurance provider
  • Taiwan map (offline or printed) – Helpful if mobile data becomes unstable
  • Refillable water bottle and snacks – Useful if stores temporarily close
  • Travel insurance – Essential coverage for weather-related delays

3. Indoor Activities During Typhoon Days

While outdoor activities become impossible during a typhoon, you can still enjoy Taiwan's indoor attractions. Shopping malls, museums, and galleries often remain open unless the situation becomes severe. Your hotel may offer amenities like gyms, pools, or spas that can help pass the time.

Indoor Options:

  • Visit shopping malls (Taipei 101, Breeze Center, Mitsui Outlet Park)
  • Explore museums (National Palace Museum, Taipei Fine Arts Museum)
  • Enjoy hotel amenities (gym, pool, spa)
  • Try Taiwan's food delivery services (often operate until conditions become dangerous)
  • Use downtime to relax, read, or plan the rest of your trip

4. Transportation in Typhoon Season

Transportation disruptions are common during Taiwan's typhoon season, especially in coastal and mountainous areas. Planning ahead can help minimize stress when travel plans change unexpectedly.

During storm alerts, public transportation may be delayed or suspended. In these situations, private transfer services like Uber or taxi services can be helpful, with door-to-door rides and professional drivers who monitor weather and road conditions in real time.
