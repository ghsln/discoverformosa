Penghu

An island archipelago famous for volcanic landscapes and ocean views.

Penghu (澎湖, Pénghú) is an archipelago in the Taiwan Strait, famous for its beaches, basalt landscapes, and maritime culture. The islands feature historic villages, lighthouses, and water-based activities such as snorkeling and fishing. With its relaxed pace, scenic beauty, and traditional festivals, Penghu provides a unique island experience distinct from Taiwan’s main island.
