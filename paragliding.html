Taiwan Paragliding Guide

Taiwan offers incredible paragliding experiences with stunning views of mountains, valleys, and coastlines. With various sites suitable for beginners to advanced pilots, the island is a perfect destination for aerial adventure enthusiasts.

Safety Tip: Always fly with licensed instructors, follow all safety briefings, and check weather conditions before your flight. Paragliding is weather-dependent, so flights may be cancelled due to strong winds or poor conditions.

Top Paragliding Spots

Soaring Paragliding, Luye, Taitung
Luye Highlands, Taitung
Beginner
Terrain: Ideal for gliding with consistent winds
Views: East Rift Valley and coastal ranges
Great for: Tandem flights with experienced instructors

One of Taiwan's most iconic spots for paragliding, located in Luye Highlands where the terrain and winds are ideal for gliding. You'll get sweeping views of the East Rift Valley and coastal ranges.

Hutoushan Lookout & 飛台灣-FlyTaiwan
Puli, Nantou
Beginner

Includes: Photos/videos services often available
Convenience: Pickup/transportation often included

Central Taiwan's popular paragliding base with gentle slopes and options for tandem flights. The site is beginner-friendly and often includes pickup/transportation and photos/videos services.

Typical Paragliding Experiences

Taiwan offers different paragliding experiences to suit various skill levels and preferences:

Tandem Flights (with Instructor)

Available across most sites for beginners, no experience needed. You'll be securely harnessed to a professional pilot who handles takeoff, flying, and landing.

Paragliding Courses & Training

If you want to learn to fly solo, there are training schools in Taiwan that offer beginner to intermediate training programs.

Tips for Paragliding in Taiwan

Make the most of your paragliding adventure with these essential tips:

Best Time & Conditions

Paragliding is weather-dependent — calm winds and clear skies are ideal. Seasons can vary by region. Luye/East Taiwan usually has consistent winds most of the year. North Coast/Yilan has coastal breezes but strong winds can cancel flights. Always check with the operator on the day of your flight.

What to Wear

Wear comfortable long pants, sneakers, sunglasses, and sunscreen. Bring a light jacket as it can be cooler at higher altitudes.

Safety & Health

Go with licensed instructors and follow all safety briefings. Inform the operator of any health concerns; some weight/age limits may apply.

Booking

Book in advance, especially during peak travel seasons or weekends. Check operator reviews and choose based on preferred scenery and experience level.
