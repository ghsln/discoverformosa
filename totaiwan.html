Ways to Get to Taiwan

Most visitors travel to Taiwan by international flight, arriving at major airports such as Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport, Taipei Songshan, Taichung, or Kaohsiung, which offer convenient connections across Asia and beyond. A smaller number of travelers arrive by sea via cruise ships or ferries that dock at ports like Keelung or Kaohsiung, providing an alternative entry option for certain routes and itineraries.

By Air

Most travelers arrive by international flights at major airports like Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport (TPE) near Taipei, Taiwan's main global gateway, and Kaohsiung International Airport (KHH) in the south, both offering direct flights from many countries. Taipei Songshan Airport (TSA) and Taichung Airport (RMQ) also handle some flights (especially regional ones), and you can easily connect onward through Taiwan's efficient transport (MRT, train, buses) to other cities.

Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport (TPE)

Main international hub near Taipei with extensive global connections.

Other Airports

Taipei Songshan (TSA), Taichung (RMQ), and Kaohsiung (KHH) handle regional and limited international flights.

By Sea

While air travel is the main entry method, some people also reach Taiwan by sea, especially on cruise ships that dock at major ports like Keelung (near Taipei) or Kaohsiung. There are also growing ferry routes to/from nearby islands and abroad, for example, a regular ferry from Xiamen to Kinmen or planned ferry services connecting Keelung with destinations like Ishigaki (Japan), offering a sea alternative for certain itineraries.
