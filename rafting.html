Taiwan Rafting Guide

Taiwan offers diverse river experiences, from thrilling whitewater rapids to gentle scenic floats. With rivers flowing through dramatic gorges and lush valleys, the island is an excellent destination for water adventure enthusiasts.

Safety Tip: Always follow guide instructions, wear provided safety gear, and check weather conditions before your trip. For whitewater rafting, some physical fitness is required, and booking with reputable operators is essential.

Top Rafting Spots

Xiuguluan River (秀姑巒溪)
Hualien
Whitewater
Best for: Whitewater Rafting
Route: 24 km from Ruisui to Changhong Bridge
Duration: 3-4 hours on the water

The most famous whitewater rafting destination in Taiwan, long river section with exciting rapids through gorges and scenic valleys.

Wutai/Lower Hayou (Hayu Creek)
Pingtung
Moderate
Experience: More Relaxed
Route: 5 km scenic stretch
Duration: About 2 hours

A gentler rafting/float experience down a scenic 5 km stretch of river, good for beginners or those who want a mix of adventure and relaxing scenery.

Baling Bridge Route
Taoyuan
Gentle
Type: Pack rafting / inflatable kayak
Suitable: Year-round and for beginners
Great for: Calmer river sections

A fun pack rafting or inflatable kayak style adventure on calmer river sections near Taoyuan, suitable year-round and for beginners.

Beishi Creek (北勢溪)
New Taipei
Gentle
Experience: Entry-level pack rafting / floating
Features: Mild rapids and scenic areas
Ideal for: Families or first-timers

Great entry-level pack rafting / floating with mild rapids and scenic areas, ideal for families or first-timers. These activities are not the same as whitewater rafting, they're more like guided river floating with paddles and generally safer and easier.

Tips for Rafting in Taiwan

Make the most of your rafting adventure with these essential tips:

Best Season

Late spring through early fall (higher water flow and more exciting rapids), though some operators run trips year-round depending on weather.

What to Bring

Sunscreen, quick-dry clothes, water shoes/sandals, change of clothes, and waterproof bag.

Safety

Tours include helmets, life jackets, and professional guides. Always follow guide instructions and check health restrictions before booking.

Booking

Many tours are available through outdoor adventure companies and travel platforms, booking in advance is wise in peak season.
