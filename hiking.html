Taiwan Hiking Guide

Taiwan offers an incredible diversity of hiking experiences, from leisurely urban trails to challenging alpine ascents. With over 200 peaks exceeding 3,000 meters, the island is a true paradise for outdoor enthusiasts.

Trail Tip: Always check weather conditions before heading out, carry sufficient water, and inform someone of your hiking plans. For high mountain hikes, permits are often required and should be applied for in advance.

Hiking Trails

Elephant Mountain (象山)
Taipei
Easy
Duration: 1-2 hours round trip
Elevation: ~183 meters
Best for: City views and photography

A short but steep climb offering the most iconic postcard view of Taipei 101 and the city. The trail consists mainly of stone steps and takes about 15-20 minutes to reach the main viewpoint.

Yangmingshan National Park (陽明山國家公園)
Taipei
Easy
Duration: 2-4 hours depending on trail
Elevation: Varies by trail
Features: Volcanic landscapes, hot springs

Offers many well-paved, gentle trails like the Qixing Mountain trail (to the main peak) with fantastic volcanic scenery and hot springs. The park features colorful flowers in spring and comfortable temperatures year-round.

Taroko Gorge Trails (太魯閣峽谷)
Hualien
Medium
Duration: 2-5 hours depending on trail
Features: Marble gorge, rivers, tunnels
Note: Helmets required in some areas

Trails like the Shakadang Trail and Baiyang Trail offer relatively flat walks with breathtaking views of marble cliffs and turquoise rivers. The gorge features some of Taiwan's most dramatic landscapes.

Alishan National Scenic Area (阿里山國家風景區)
Chiayi County
Medium
Duration: Half-day to full day
Features: Ancient forests, sunrise views
Access: Forest railway available

Famous for its sea of clouds, ancient cypress forests, and sunrise views over the mountains, accessible via forest railways and trails. The area is particularly magical at sunrise when the clouds form an ocean-like vista.

Yushan Main Peak (玉山主峰)
Yushan National Park
Hard
Duration: 2 days typically
Elevation: 3,952 meters
Accommodation: Mountain lodge available

The highest peak in Northeast Asia at 3,952 meters. Requires a permit and good physical fitness, but the "roof of Taiwan" view is unparalleled. The hike typically takes 2 days with an overnight stay at Paiyun Lodge.

Permit Required: Apply well in advance through Yushan National Park website
Xue Mountain (Snow Mountain) (雪山)
Shei-Pa National Park
Hard
Duration: 2-3 days typically
Elevation: 3,886 meters
Highlights: Holy Ridge, cirque landscape

Taiwan's second-highest peak, famous for the stunning "Holy Ridge" and the cirque known as Snow Mountain's Main Peak. The trail features diverse landscapes including forests, alpine lakes, and dramatic ridgelines.

Permit Required: Apply through Shei-Pa National Park administration

High Mountain Preparation: For hard hikes, proper acclimatization is essential to avoid altitude sickness. Bring warm clothing regardless of season, as temperatures can drop below freezing at high elevations. Always hike with a partner and carry emergency supplies.
