Taiwan's Potential World Heritage Treasures

While Taiwan is not a member state of UNESCO and therefore cannot have sites on the official World Heritage List, it boasts a rich collection of cultural and natural landmarks of "Outstanding Universal Value." The Taiwanese government has identified a tentative list of sites that represent the island's unique heritage, from majestic natural landscapes to profound historical and cultural landmarks. These destinations are already treasured locally and are more than worthy of a visit for any traveler seeking to understand the soul of Taiwan.

Note: All sites mentioned are recognized by the Taiwanese government as having potential Outstanding Universal Value and are protected under Taiwan's domestic cultural and natural heritage laws.

Notable Heritage Sites

Taroko National Park
Hualien
Admission: $200 NTD
Opening Hours: 24 hours
Phone: 03 862 1100

Taroko National Park, on Taiwan's east coast, is famous for the breathtaking, marble-walled Taroko Gorge carved by the Liwu River, featuring towering cliffs, lush forests, and turquoise waters. A biodiversity hotspot with high peaks, diverse habitats, and numerous hiking trails like Swallow Grotto and Tunnel of Nine Turns, it's a major natural wonder offering stunning scenery, waterfalls (like Baiyang), and cultural sites such as the Eternal Spring Shrine.

The Alishan Forest Railway
Chiayi
Admission: $198 NTD
Opening Hours: 08:30 AM - 04:30 PM
Phone: 05 267 9200

The Alishan Forest Railway is a historic, narrow-gauge mountain railway in Chiayi, Taiwan, renowned for its engineering marvels, stunning scenery, and rich history as a former logging route. It is a popular tourist attraction, passing through three climate zones (tropical, subtropical, and temperate) as it climbs from Chiayi City (elevation 30 m) to the Alishan mountain area (over 2,000 m).

Yushan National Park
Nantou
Admission: Free (Need permit to enter)
Opening Hours: 24 hours
Phone: 04 9277 3121

Yushan National Park is a protected alpine region in the heart of Taiwan, famous for containing Yushan (Mount Jade), the highest peak in Northeast Asia at 3,952 meters (12,966 ft). The park is a haven for hikers and a critical ecological sanctuary.

Penghu Columnar Basalt
Penghu
Admission: Free
Opening Hours: 24 hours
Phone: 06 993 3082

Penghu's columnar basalts are spectacular geological formations of tightly packed, often hexagonal, volcanic rock columns created when ancient lava flows cooled and cracked, forming impressive cliffs and unique landscapes like the "pipe organ" basalt at Jishanyu or the easily accessible walls at Daguoyue, a protected natural wonder in Taiwan.

Kinmen Islands
Kinmen
Largest City: Jincheng
Specialties: Kaoliang Liqour, Gong Tang
Get There: 1h 5m from Taoyuan

The Kinmen Islands (or Quemoy) are a Taiwanese-governed archipelago near mainland China, famous for their unique blend of stunning natural scenery, distinct traditional architecture (like ancient homes and European-style buildings from wealthy Chinese), rich history as a Cold War military front, and unique culture, including shell-crafted knives and famous Kaoliang liquor.

Orchid Island
Taitung
Ethnic Groups: Tao, Han
Specialties: Flying fish culture
Get There: 1h 5m from Taoyuan

Orchid Island (Lanyu), known to its indigenous inhabitants as "Pongso no Tao" or "Island of the People," is a remote volcanic island off Taiwan's southeastern coast and home to the Tao (Yami) people. The Tao have a distinctive and well-preserved maritime culture centered around fishing and agriculture, which is unique among Taiwan's indigenous groups.
