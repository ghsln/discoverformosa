Luxury Accommodations in Taiwan

Taiwan offers a diverse range of luxury accommodations, from urban hotels in bustling Taipei to serene resorts in breathtaking natural settings. Each property provides unique experiences that blend international standards with local Taiwanese hospitality, ensuring unforgettable stays for discerning travelers.

Note: Each accommodation featured below offers distinctive amenities, locations, and experiences. Whether you're seeking urban sophistication, natural tranquility, or architectural beauty, Taiwan's luxury hotels deliver exceptional service and memorable stays.

Taipei, Songshan District

Mandarin Oriental, Taipei

Located in Taipei's Songshan District, the Mandarin Oriental offers an opulent European-style experience that stands out in the city's skyline. This hotel is a destination in itself, featuring a 20-meter heated outdoor pool and a lavish spa spanning 3,500 square meters.

Food enthusiasts will be delighted by the six on-site restaurants and lounges, including the Michelin-starred Ya Ge, which serves exquisite Cantonese cuisine and dim sum. The hotel's elegant rooms are equipped with marble bathrooms and walk-in closets, ensuring a stay defined by sophistication and comfort.

Taipei, Xinyi District

W Taipei

For a vibrant and trendy stay in the heart of Taipei's Xinyi District, the W Taipei is the place to be. This hotel embodies a hip, high-energy vibe with an artsy interior, epic city views, and some rooms that directly face the iconic Taipei 101.

The experience begins in the lively 10th-floor lobby, which is home to the popular Woobar. You can enjoy the pool area, which often features DJs, or indulge in the excellent dining at Yen Chinese Restaurant. Its direct access to underground tunnels connecting to major shopping malls also makes it incredibly convenient for explorers.

Hualien County, Taroko Gorge

Silks Place, Taroko Gorge

Nestled in the magnificent Taroko Gorge in Hualien County, Silks Place is the perfect base for exploring one of Taiwan's most stunning natural wonders. This hotel combines luxury with nature, offering a serene escape surrounded by towering peaks and waterfalls.

After a day of hiking, you can unwind in the gorge-facing swimming pool or rejuvenate in the hotel's wellness spa. The tranquil atmosphere and breathtaking scenery provide a uniquely Taiwanese luxury experience that focuses on relaxation and connection with the natural world.

Nantou County, Sun Moon Lake

The Lalu, Sun Moon Lake

Situated on the shores of the picturesque Sun Moon Lake in Nantou County, The Lalu is a masterpiece of minimalist design by renowned Australian architect Kerry Hill. The resort offers an atmosphere of serene luxury, with some villas featuring private, temperature-controlled swimming pools.

Designed by renowned Australian architect Kerry Hill, The Lalu showcases minimalist architecture that harmonizes with the natural landscape. Guests can enjoy stunning lake views, explore the property's unique "Scenic Steps" inspired by the Great Wall, or indulge in a soothing treatment at the Lalu Spa.

Kaohsiung, Asia New Bay Area

Silks Club, Kaohsiung

In the southern port city of Kaohsiung, the Silks Club offers a sophisticated boutique experience focused on art and design. From the moment you enter the lobby, you're greeted by the mesmerizing "Dancing Particles" motion art sculpture, setting the tone for a creatively inspired stay.

The hotel is located in the Asia New Bay Area, putting its modern attractions within easy reach. Known for its personalized service and stylish ambiance. The Silks Club provides a more intimate and curated luxury experience compared to larger hotel chains, making it a favorite for discerning travelers.
