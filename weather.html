Taiwan's Climate

Taiwan has a subtropical climate with distinct seasonal variations. The island experiences warm temperatures year-round, but with significant differences between seasons. Northern Taiwan tends to be cooler and rainier than the south, while mountainous areas can be much colder, with occasional snowfall in winter. Understanding Taiwan's weather patterns will help you plan the perfect trip based on your preferences for activities and comfort.

Travel Tip: Taiwan's weather can be unpredictable, especially during seasonal transitions. Always check the local forecast before heading out and be prepared with appropriate clothing for sudden changes.

Seasonal Guide

Spring
March - May
18-25°C (64-77°F)
Cherry blossoms season, comfortable for hiking
Light jacket recommended, occasional rain

Spring is one of the most beautiful times to visit Taiwan. The weather is mild and comfortable, with blooming flowers throughout the island. Cherry blossoms are the main attraction during this season, with popular viewing spots like Yangmingshan National Park and Alishan. It's the perfect time for outdoor activities like hiking and exploring Taiwan's natural beauty without the intense heat of summer.

Summer
June - August
28-35°C (82-95°F)
Hot and humid, typhoon season begins
Beach weather, light clothing, umbrella essential

Summer in Taiwan is hot, humid, and rainy, especially in the south. This is typhoon season, with occasional heavy storms typically occurring between July and September. Despite the heat and humidity, summer is a great time for beach activities, water sports, and visiting Taiwan's many night markets. The east coast and islands like Penghu and Green Island are particularly popular during this season.

Fall
September - November
20-28°C (68-82°F)
Ideal travel season, minimal rainfall
Perfect for outdoor activities and festivals

Fall is considered by many to be the best time to visit Taiwan. The weather is pleasant with comfortable temperatures, low humidity, and minimal rainfall. This is an excellent season for outdoor activities like hiking, cycling, and exploring Taiwan's national parks. Numerous festivals take place during fall, including the Mid-Autumn Festival and Taiwan's National Day celebrations.

Winter
December - February
13-18°C (55-64°F)
Cool and dry, snow in high mountains
Warm layers needed, especially in northern Taiwan

Winter in Taiwan is cool and relatively dry, though northern areas can be rainy. Temperatures are mild compared to many countries, but the lack of central heating in most buildings can make it feel colder. High mountain areas like Hehuanshan and Yushan may experience snowfall, attracting visitors for winter scenery. Hot springs are particularly popular during this season, with numerous resorts throughout the island.
