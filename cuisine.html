Classic & Iconic Dishes

Classic Dish

Beef Noodle Soup
(牛肉麵)

Taiwan's national dish: hearty broth, tender beef, and chewy noodles.

Classic Dish

Braised Pork Rice
(滷肉飯/Lu Rou Fan)

Comfort food staple: savory minced pork over rice.

Classic Dish

Xiao Long Bao
(小籠包)

Steamed soup dumplings with juicy pork inside; famous at places like Din Tai Fung.

Classic Dish

Dan-Zai Noodles
(擔仔麵)

Tainan specialty: light broth noodles topped with minced pork and shrimp.

Street Food & Night Market Favorites

Street Food

Oyster Omelette
(蚵仔煎)

Chewy, savory, with a sweet-savory sauce.

Street Food

Taiwanese Fried Chicken
(鹹酥雞/炸雞排)

Crispy, flavorful, often seasoned with basil and pepper salt.

Street Food

Gua Bao
(刈包)

"Taiwanese hamburger": braised pork belly in a soft steamed bun with pickles and peanuts.

Street Food

Pepper Buns
(胡椒餅)

Hot and juicy baked buns with peppery pork filling (especially at Raohe Night Market).

Street Food

Oyster Vermicelli
(蚵仔麵線)

Thick noodle soup with oysters (and sometimes pig intestine).

Street Food

Stinky Tofu
(臭豆腐)

Fermented tofu snack with a strong aroma but delicious fried texture.

Street Food

Ba Wan
(肉圓)

Large chewy dumpling made with sticky rice flour and savory filling.

Street Food

Coffin Bread
(棺材板)

Unique Tainan snack: a hollowed bread bowl filled with creamy stew.

Drinks & Desserts

Drink

Bubble Tea
(珍珠奶茶/Boba)

Taiwan's world-famous tea drink with chewy tapioca pearls.

Dessert

Shaved Ice
(剉冰/Mango Shaved Ice)

Refreshing dessert topped with fruit, condensed milk, and toppings.

Dessert

Pineapple Cake
(鳳梨酥)

Sweet pastry with pineapple jam — great as a snack or gift.

Breakfast & Everyday Eats

Breakfast

Dan Bing
(蛋餅)

Classic Taiwanese breakfast food - savory egg crepe with various fillings.

Breakfast

Scallion Pancakes
(蔥抓餅)

Crispy, flaky pancakes with scallions - a popular breakfast or snack.

Breakfast

Soy Milk & Youtiao
(豆漿+油條)

Traditional Taiwanese breakfast combo. Hot soy milk with crispy fried dough sticks.
