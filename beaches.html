Beaches in Taiwan

While not as famous as other Southeast Asian destinations, Taiwan has beautiful and diverse coastlines. From golden sand beaches perfect for swimming and water sports to coral island paradises ideal for snorkeling, Taiwan offers a variety of beach experiences for every type of traveler.

Most-visited Beaches in Taiwan

Fulong Beach

New Taipei City

Famous for its golden sand and the annual Ho-Hai-Yan Rock Festival. The sandbar creates a calm swimming area perfect for families and swimmers of all levels. Located in New Taipei City, it's easily accessible from Taipei.

Baishawan

New Taipei City

A beautiful white sand bay with clear water, popular for swimming and water sports. Located in New Taipei City, Baishawan offers stunning views and excellent facilities for beachgoers looking for a relaxing day by the sea.

Kenting National Park

Pingtung

Taiwan's beach capital with Nanwan (South Bay) and Baisha (White Sand Bay) as top spots for sunbathing, swimming, and partying with a vibrant nightlife scene. This tropical paradise in Pingtung offers year-round beach enjoyment.

Xiaoliuqiu

Pingtung

A coral island paradise perfect for snorkeling and swimming with sea turtles. Located off the coast of Pingtung, this small island offers crystal clear waters and abundant marine life, making it a favorite for underwater enthusiasts.

Beach Spots & Activities

Sunbathing & Relaxing

Just chill on the sand, read a book, nap, or enjoy the sunshine and sea breeze at beaches like 白沙灣/Baishawan or 福隆/Fulong.

Swimming & Wading

On calmer beaches (especially in the northeast like Fulong), you can swim or paddle in the water, keep in mind currents can be strong at many Taiwan beaches, so check local conditions.

Building Sandcastle

Great for families and kids! Play beach volleyball, frisbee, or just build sandcastles on the wide sandy stretches.

Shell Collecting & Rock Standing

Many beaches have shells and interesting stones for collecting or creative stacking. Qixingtan's pebble shore is famous for this.
