Snow in Taiwan

Finding snow in subtropical Taiwan is a special experience, primarily available in the high mountains during winter.

Where to Find Snow

Hehuanshan (合歡山)

View on Maps

The most accessible high-mountain area, where snowfall in winter (Dec-Feb) can be heavy enough for sledding and even snow viewing festivals. The Hehuan Pass is a popular spot.

Yushan (Jade Mountain) National Park

View on Maps

The peaks here, including Yushan itself, are often capped with snow in the winter months.

Snow Mountain (Xueshan)

View on Maps

As the name implies, it's one of the most reliable places for snowfall.

Snow Activities

Go snow-viewing and take photos

Head to high mountain areas like Hehuanshan and other peaks to watch snow fall or cover the landscape and capture winter scenery. These spots are popular because snow is rare in most of Taiwan.

Play in the snow

When snow actually falls, you can build snowmen, have snowball fights, and enjoy the unique winter feel on open snowy ground.

Hike snowy trails

If you're prepared and conditions allow, winter hikes on snowy mountain paths offer a scenic adventure (especially at higher elevations).

Warm up with cozy activities nearby

After snowy fun, relax with hot drinks, local winter foods, or hot springs in nearby towns to warm up from the cold. (Typical winter travel tip; snow locations pair well with warm treats.)
