Water Skiing in Taiwan Guide

Water skiing isn't as common as surfing or rafting in Taiwan, but it is available, mostly at controlled water venues and coastal recreation areas. These spots are beginner-friendly and usually run by professional operators.

Safety Tip: Always follow instructor instructions, wear provided safety gear, and check weather conditions before your session. For beginners, cable parks are the safest option as they provide stable conditions and professional instruction.

Main Places to Try Water Skiing

Bitan Scenic Area (碧潭)
New Taipei City
Cable System
Type: Cable water skiing / tow system
Best for: Beginners & first-timers
Why: Calm water, controlled environment

One of the most popular and accessible places to try water skiing near Taipei. Calm river water with a controlled environment, safety-focused. Wakeboarding is often available here too.

Lotus Wake Park
Kaohsiung
Wake Park
Type: Cable wake park (water ski & wakeboard)
Best for: Beginners → intermediate riders
Why: Stable conditions, professional instruction

A cable wake park offering water skiing and wakeboarding. Features stable conditions, professional instruction, and gear rental. Great if you want to learn safely without open-sea waves.

Coastal Resorts
Kenting / Penghu
Boat-Towed
Type: Boat-towed water skiing
Best for: Those with prior experience
Note: Seasonal & weather dependent

Boat-towed water skiing at coastal resorts in Kenting and Penghu. More scenic than cable parks, but availability depends heavily on weather and season. Less predictable than cable parks.

Here's what you can typically expect when water skiing in Taiwan:

10–15 minutes per run

Instruction

Instructor included (especially for beginners)

Equipment

Skis, life jacket, helmet provided

Skill Level

No experience required at cable parks

Beginner Tips for Water Skiing

Make the most of your water skiing adventure with these essential tips:

Cable Parks First

Choose cable parks for first attempts. They provide stable conditions and are much easier for beginners than open-water boat towing.

Proper Attire

Wear tight-fitting swimwear that won't get in the way. Avoid loose clothing that could catch on equipment.

Follow Instructions

Listen closely to hand signals and instructions from your guide or instructor. They'll teach you proper form and safety procedures.

Starting Position

Start with knees bent, arms straight. Let the boat or cable pull you up rather than trying to stand up too early.

Expect to Fall

Falling is normal and part of the learning process. Everyone falls when learning to water ski!
