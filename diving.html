Why Dive in Taiwan

Taiwan is surrounded by clear ocean waters and offers a rich variety of underwater experiences — from shallow reef dives and coral gardens to deeper ocean walls and wrecks. The island's ocean currents and geography create diverse marine life, colorful corals, and multiple dive sites for all skill levels.

Taiwan Today: Whether you're a complete beginner or an advanced diver, Taiwan has options ranging from shore dives near major cities to boat dives around remote islands.

Top Diving & Snorkeling Spots in Taiwan

Green Island (綠島)
Taitung County
All Levels
One of Taiwan's most popular dive destinations
Excellent visibility & abundant coral reefs
Known areas: Shilang and Dabaisha

Great for both snorkeling and scuba diving. Water clarity and marine diversity make it ideal for reef exploration.

Xiaoliuqiu (小琉球)
Pingtung County
Beginner-Friendly
Famous for sea turtles
Abundant tropical fish & calm waters
Sites: Vase Rock and Lobster Cave

A small island famous for its sea turtles, abundant tropical fish, and calm waters. Suitable for beginners to intermediate divers.

Kenting & Hengchun Peninsula
Kenting/Hengchun
All Levels
Southern Taiwan's tropical waters
Year-round diving hotspot
Sites: Wanlitong and Houbihu

Coral reefs, sandy plains, and fish schools make it a great all-round dive destination. Southern Taiwan's tropical waters make this area a year-round diving hotspot.

Penghu (澎湖群島)
Penghu Archipelago
Intermediate
Striking underwater scenery & coral gardens
High visibility
Includes South Penghu Marine National Park

An archipelago with striking underwater scenery, coral gardens, and high visibility. The South Penghu Marine National Park includes coral reefs and marine habitats. Excellent for both snorkeling and scuba.

Northeast Coast & North Taiwan
Near Taipei
Beginner-Friendly
Accessible by short trip from Taipei
Sites: Longdong Bay, Bat Cave, Chaojing Park
Popular year-round dive sites

Accessible by a short trip from Taipei, this area includes dive spots such as Longdong Bay (popular year-round), Bat Cave and Secret Garden (interesting spots for frequent dives), and Chaojing Park (fun local dive area for both beginners and fun dives).

Diving Tips

Make the most of your diving adventure in Taiwan with these essential tips:

Certified Operators

Always dive with a certified operator and guide. 台灣潛水TaiwanDiveCenter and other licensed operators ensure safety and quality experiences.

Weather Conditions

Check local weather and sea conditions before going out. Taiwan's weather can change quickly, especially in coastal areas.

Equipment

Many dive shops include equipment and tanks — confirm what's included in your package before booking.

Transportation

Some remote dive spots require boat transport. Plan accordingly and confirm transportation details with your operator.

Respect Marine Life

Respect marine life and coral — maintain good buoyancy control and avoid touching or disturbing underwater creatures.
