Your Taiwan Power Adapter Guide

Before you pack, make sure you can power your devices. Taiwan uses a 110V electrical system, which is different from many countries.

Electrical Specifications

  • Voltage: 110V / 60 Hz
  • Plug Types: Type A (two flat pins) and Type B (two flat pins with a round ground pin)

Do You Need an Adapter?

No Adapter Needed:

Travelers from the US, Canada, Mexico, and Japan: Your plugs are compatible.

Adapter Required:

Travelers from Europe, the UK, Australia, Africa, and most of Asia: You will need a plug adapter.

Internet in Taiwan: SIM Cards, WiFi & Connectivity

Staying online in Taiwan is easy and affordable. The country has excellent, high-speed internet infrastructure.

Your Best Options for Internet Access:

1. SIM Card (Most Popular & Recommended)

Why: Get a local number and high-speed data immediately.

Where: Counters at Taoyuan International Airport (TPE) after you land. Major providers are Chunghwa Telecom, FarEasTone, and Taiwan Mobile.

What to Get: Tourist SIM packages offer unlimited data for 3, 5, 7, 10, or 30 days. They are cost-effective and easy to set up.

2. eSIM (The Digital & Convenient Choice)

Why: Instant activation before you even landâ€”no physical SIM to swap.

How: Check if your phone is eSIM-compatible. You can purchase a Taiwan data plan online from providers like Airalo or AIS eSIM before your trip.

3. Pocket WiFi (WiFi Router)

Why: Perfect for families or groups who want to connect multiple devices (phones, laptops, tablets) to a single source.

How: Rent a device at the airport or have it delivered to your hotel. Remember to keep it charged.

4. Free Public WiFi

iTaiwan: The government-provided free WiFi service. You need to register once (with a passport number or Facebook account). It's available in airports, MRT stations, train stations, and some convenience stores. Speeds are suitable for messaging and maps.

Convenience Stores: 7-Eleven (7-WiFi) and FamilyMart also offer free WiFi, though registration may be required.

Our Recommendation:

For a solo traveler, a tourist SIM card from the airport is the simplest and most reliable option. For a tech-savvy traveler with a compatible phone, an eSIM is the most convenient.
